Preface

Music has a long and rich history, but in the last 50 years we have created plentiful and diverse songs and genres.
Each genre and song has its unique features, and we aim to visualize and explore the main differences and understand the data.
The original dataset is the Million Song Dataset(MSD), from in The Laboratory for the Recognition and Organization of Speech and Audio (LabROSA).

We obtained a sample with 10,000 songs, from which we extracted the track ID, year of release, artist ID,
tempo, key, genre and loudness for each track.
We also obtained an additional dataset() that contains the same track IDs and corresponding lyrics for the tracks; more specifically,
how many times a word was observed in a given song.

We aim to combine this dataset with the original dataset to make more meaningful observations about the tracks and their content.

Visualisation of the two PCAs

We used principal component analysis on the words(see bubble chart) and the MSD variables(see scatter-plots) with the intention of exploring underlying patterns in each dataset,if any.
That's because PCA can show use how similar the songs are to each other
For the first PCA plot, five features were extracted: artist familiarity, artist hotness, the duration of the song, the loudness and also the tempo.
For the second one, there are 5000 different words and their counts in each song. 19.5% of the variance is captured by PC1 and 5.9% by PC2. For the multidimension scaling we used the first 2 PCs. Same as previous plot, the data points were colored by genre. We did multidimensional scaling on the first two principal components (PCs). PC1 captures 91% and PC2 captures 8 % of the variance. The rest of the components can be dropped safely without losing any information.
